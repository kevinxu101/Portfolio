Reflections on College Life

Short but sweet.

The sudden shift from face to face classes into purely online curriculum was nightmarish. My productivity and drive to simply to do anything at all diminished significantly. Being stuck in all four corners of my house does affect my mental state greatly. I appreciate all the people who have made our stay bearable, those who have taught me new things and hobbies and for those who wished me to strive harder into greater heights.

Speaking for my career, I do only have a simple and humble vision, a working career with a proper work-life balance. While I still have the passion and drive to develop things, I wish I could proceed slowly and sustainably, I wish not to burnout on things I loved the most.

I could still work a few years of intense developer workload while I am still young, but along the way, you will age and things will definitely get into you, thus I might decide to go into more advisory/consultancy roles for companies.

There are still lots of college moments I believe this pandemic have taken away from us, but we have to move onwards, so we could make our tomorrow something to look forward.

My Projects

Truly made with love. Here are some of my projects that were made for school projects or done in my free time.

CharitAble

Laravel - HTML , CSS, JS
A crowdsourcing platform to provide donors with greater levels of transparency through programs, donations integration with blockchain and automated withdrawals for charities.

APIs that were used are the following: PayPal, Paymongo for donations, Wise for automated charity program withdrawal, and lastly, Openzeppelin in conjunction with Web3 and Solidity to integrate any donation/withdrawal transaction to the blockchain.
